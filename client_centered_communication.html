Overview

Client-Centered Communication is a microlearning experience created for a flagship development program at Knowledge Group Consulting (KGC). It aligns to CMI and ICF standards so that behaviors practiced in the module map cleanly to recognized competencies. Working with a CMI/ICF-versed SME, I translated program objectives into concise, scenario-driven practice that builds confidence in real client conversations.

My role

SME collaboration, content mapping, learning design, Rise 360 build, QA & accessibility, SCORM packaging, LMS interation

Objectives

Demonstrate active listening, surface needs with effective questions, and structure responses that move the conversation forward

What learners practice

  • Active listening & empathy: reflecting language, acknowledging constraints, and checking understanding.
  • Powerful questioning: opening, probing, and calibrating questions tied to CMI/ICF coaching behaviors.
  • Structuring a response: framing options, summarizing trade-offs, and agreeing next steps.
  • Handling resistance: recognizing concerns, reframing, and keeping momentum without pressure.

Design approach

  • Micro-first flow: short segments with a single behavior target—easy to take between client calls.
  • Scenario blocks: realistic client prompts with branched feedback that references the specific behavior standard.
  • Worked examples: “good / better” model responses contrasted with common pitfalls to accelerate pattern-building.
  • Retrieval practice: quick checks and re-tries that reinforce cues without breaking momentum.
  • Job aids: a conversation checklist and a “probing questions” prompt card for on-the-job use.

Build & delivery

  • Authoring: Articulate Rise 360 (custom theme, scenario/knowledge-check blocks, labelled graphics).
  • Standards: packaged as SCORM for LMS tracking; content structure is xAPI-ready if needed.
  • Deployment: imported to the LMS as part of the broader program pathway; completion contributes to overall progression.
  • Accessibility: color contrast, keyboard navigation, focus states, alt text, and clear link labels (WCAG-informed).
  • QA: SME validation against CMI/ICF descriptors, device/browser testing, and reporting checks in the LMS.

Early indicators

  • Higher completion vs. longer modules in the same pathway; learners cite “use-now” relevance.
  • Reduced time-to-first-use for target behaviors due to tight scenario → feedback loops.

Deliverables

  • Outcome mapping aligned to CMI/ICF competencies
  • Storyboard and interaction scripts
  • Voice & tone guidance for prompts and feedback
  • Accessibility notes, media specs, and QA checklist
  • SCORM package and LMS handover guide
