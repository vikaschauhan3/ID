Panasonic Crossfire Contest

Overview

Panasonic India wanted a scalable way to lift product knowledge, competitive awareness, policy/process clarity, and practical selling skills across a large channel sales workforce. Instead of a traditional course, we designed the Crossfire Contest—a nationwide, multi-round competition where employees learned, qualified, and competed for cash prizes and national recognition. The contest format drove motivation and sustained participation while giving leadership clear, comparable evidence of capability growth.

Program Format (Multi-round)

  • Rounds 1–2 (Online): timed objective assessments covering products, competitors, policies, and sales skills. Question banks were shared in advance to promote self-learning and consistent prep.
  • Round 3 (Zone level): written assessment, role-plays with scoring rubrics, product demonstration briefs, and scenario-based activities.
  • Finals (National): live, game-show style quiz with multiple rounds—individual questions, rapid fire, buzzer rounds, and extempore—staged in front of senior leadership with a professional presenter.

Outcomes & Impact

  • High engagement: competition mechanics (leaderboards, qualification, prizes) created pull for learning across regions and branches.
  • Measured capability growth: progressive assessments and rubrics produced comparable scores for selection, recognition, and targeted coaching.
  • Actionable insights: item-level analysis highlighted policy/process and competitive knowledge gaps to address with ongoing micro-content and monthly testing.

My Role & Contributions (Lead Instructional Designer)

  • Program design: co-created the competition framework to self-motivate participants to learn and demonstrate knowledge/skills.
  • Assessment banks (Rounds 1–2): authored item banks; shared prep materials in advance to promote self-learning; tuned difficulty and coverage.
  • Nationwide administration: worked with IT teams to schedule and run online tests across India; monitored delivery and resolved issues.
  • Analytics & scoring: consolidated results for all participants; produced scores used for eliminations and advancement.
  • Practical assessments (Round 3): designed written tasks, role-play scenarios, product demo briefs, and detailed scoring rubrics.
  • Final quiz design: created the show flow and question sets (individual, rapid fire, buzzer, extempore); prepared presenter scripts and run-of-show.
  • Event execution: supported end-to-end delivery—content, presenter prep, judge briefings, participant management, and on-site coordination.

Key Deliverables

  • Assessment item banks and prep packs (Rounds 1–2); scoring sheets and analytics exports.
  • Role-play scenarios, demo briefs, assessment rubrics, and learning activities (Round 3).
  • Finals quiz: round formats, question banks, presenter script, judging guide, and event collateral.
  • Post-program report and recommendations for continued learning.
