Overview

ADSG Innovation Labs was a government innovation program that empowered Abu Dhabi public-sector teams to design, test, and deliver citizen-centered improvements. The Labs combine practical training with facilitated sprints so departments can move from ideas to validated proofs of concept quickly and collaboratively.

KGC partnered with ADSG on the program. This video is a short showcase of the Labs’ goals, approach, and outcomes.

What the program covers

  • Service-improvement opportunities in the public sector (inter-department effectiveness, employee & citizen happiness, new service models).
  • Innovation methods such as Design Thinking, SCAMPER, Brainwriting, Mind-mapping, rapid prototyping, and a light framework for assessing the benefits and risks of pilot projects.
  • Clear links from ideas to measurable results and handover into day-to-day operations.

Approach

  • Script & structure: Distilled program insights into a clear, short text based narrative highlighting the Labs’ goals, methods, and outcomes.
  • Storyboard: Planned scene, motion graphics, beats and on-screen cues to keep the story engaging, inspiring and connected through the scenes.
  • Animation & design (Vyond): Built all scenes, iconography, motion, transitions—and tuned pacing for quick comprehension.
  • Finishing & delivery: Added arabic text (translations from english), SFX/music, and final packaging for sharing program highlights.

Panasonic India — Crossfire Contest

A nationwide, competition-based learning program to upskill and motivate channel sales teams, culminating in cash prizes and national recognition.

  • Competition-based Learning
  • Sales Enablement
  • Assessment & Coaching

Brief

Panasonic India needed a scalable way to strengthen product, policy, and competitive knowledge and improve selling skills across a large channel sales organization. We designed a contest-driven learning journey that mixed online assessments, live simulations, and on-stage finals—tying learning to recognition and rewards. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Scale & Coverage

  • Pan-India, 27 branches covering multiple regions (North, South, East, West). :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • 413 participants across roles: Regional/Branch Managers, Area Managers, Junior Sales Officers. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Departments: Consumer Products, Air-Conditioning, Digital Imaging. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Format

  1. Level 1–2 (Branch, Online): timed objective assessments (product, competition, policy, selling skills). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  2. Level 3 (Zone, In-person): written subjective test, role-plays, major/minor product demos, group presentations. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  3. Level 4 (National Finals): analytics + planning activities (Tower of Hanoi, Desert Survival), product demo, role-plays, on-stage quiz & extempore. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

What made it different

  • Competition as motivation: cash prizes + national recognition for top performers; broad participation with progressive qualification. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Learning + measurement together: multi-round design blended knowledge checks, simulations, and real presentation skills. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Actionable insights: item-level analysis identified gaps—policy/process and competitive knowledge—feeding future “knowledge nuggets” and monthly testing. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Outcomes

The program was well-received and created strong pull for ongoing learning, with clear recommendations for a sustained sales academy approach (monthly knowledge tests, competition education packs, process/policy micro-content, and on-the-job coaching). :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Result: a successful, high-engagement alternative to conventional training that celebrated knowledge and skills publicly—driving adoption across branches. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
